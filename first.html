Heroes who saved hundreds of lives as heavy rains wreaked havoc in north India

The rescue and relief teams along with the police have saved over 1000 people and cattle in Himachal Pradesh during the recent flash floods triggered by the torrential rains. Punjab police and NDRF teams rescued over 491 people in Punjab alone. Numerous visuals of police, the NDRF and SDRF teams risking their own lives to save trapped or stranded individuals in floods and rain-affected areas have been doing the rounds on social media.The northern parts of the country are reeling under severe flood-like situations with landslides in hill states, and swollen and overflowing rivers in the region. The national and state disaster relief teams and the police force have saved over 1000 people and cattle in Himachal Pradesh during the recent flash floods triggered by the torrential rains. Punjab police and NDRF teams rescued over 491 people in Punjab alone. Sanjeev Kumar, a Punjab civil services officer posted as SDM, Khamano, Fatehgarh Sahib, jumped into flood waters near Gurdwara Sahib Bibangarh on Monday to save the life of a 26-year-old man who had climbed to a pole to save his life and was crying for help. The rescue was not easy as the flood water had risen up to 12 feet. The officer was seen removing his clothes in a jiffy and swimming to save the life of the man. A 2019 batch Himachal cadre IPS officer, Mayank Chaudhary, is also in the news for his heroic deed to save the stranded trekkers in the Chandratal area of Lahaul and Spiti."He stood tall in 4ft snow at Chandertal for the last three days and nights. He kept everyone in high spirits and left last after ensuring the evacuation of all 266 tourists. Proud of you Mayank," Himachal DGP Satwant Atwal said.
